Rotur Economy

Overview

Rotur has its own economy, thanks to its own currency - Rotur credits. Rotur credits are often exchanged between users for services, such as art commissions or special requests for any service or game a user may be working on. Some parts of Rotur offer things for credits, such as changing your banner or subscribing to Rotur Lite.

How to earn credits

There are various ways to earn credits, though some are more effective than others. Listed below are some of the most notable methods people in the Rotur community use to earn credits:

Daily Credits

Daily credits are the most common way people earn credits, and for many users, is their only source of credit income. There are 2 kinds of daily credits: One given out by Rotur itself, and another one given out by being active in the originChats origin server. Depending on what subscription tier you have, you may get more credits per day. On top of subscription multipliers, there are also wealth multipliers which actually decrease the amount of daily credits you earn per day. All multipliers are:

  • Drive: ×2
  • Pro or Max: ×3
  • Having 500+ credits: ×0.5
  • Having 1000+ credits: ×0

Effectiveness: Can be slow, but is consistent.

Economy Keys

This method is the way the Rotur docs recommends is the most effective way. However, in actuality, the effectiveness of this method can vary based on how popular the product a given key is associated with is.

Devfund

You can complete any of the petitions found on Rotur's devfund page, with each petition ranging from around 50-150 credits. However, to claim the accumulated funds associated with a devfund petition, you may have to contact Mistium, as only she has the power to do that.

Developing Origin / Rotur apps

When publishing an app to Appsie, you can choose to have it be a free app, or a paid app.
Effectiveness: not very effective

Items

When you create an item, you can choose to sell that item for credits. Since items don't really do anything outside of providing bragging rights (unlike keys), this method is very ineffective.

Quick-time events

Being in the right place at the right time is a very good way to earn credits. An example is claiming a Rotur gift, being there for a giveaway, or charging credits whenever someone suggests something for your product.
Effectiveness: Varies, since while large sums can be involved, quick-time events are infrequent.

System taxes

Every time an account under a Rotur system claims a daily credit, whether that be from Rotur itself or the chat credit in originChats, the owner of that Rotur system gets 0.25 credits each time. If a system owner claims the daily credit themselves, they don't get the 0.25 credit bonus.
Effectiveness: Slow, but consistent.

Asking for credits

This is the method the developer of Rotur Assistant personally doesn't recommend, but it can work if the person you ask is in the right mood. Even if it does have the chance of working, please do not rely on this method.
Effectiveness: Varies, but may instead make people avoid giving credits to you altogether, and may result in a punishment.
