Overview

Rotur is an ecosystem created by Mistium (aliases: Mist, Sophie). It is home to various services (see list of services). It is also home to a relatively small active community, consisting of about 100-200+ active users, despite the much higher number of registered Rotur accounts. Similar to services like Microsoft and Google, all you need is one unified account to use all Rotur services. Some services may offer additional benefits for people who pay for a Rotur subscription. History

Rotur started as an idea from a user named ThatBeaverDev. Since then, Rotur was founded by Mistium and co-founded by ThatBeaverDev. Rotur started in June of 2024 initially as a way to handle accounts for originOS. A couple of months after Rotur came into fruition, ThatBeaverDev would silently distance himself from being on the Rotur Team, though he would still stick around in the Rotur community. Without originOS, Rotur wouldn't exist today. Both Rotur and originOS were created by the same person: Mistium. As a result of this, some features of originOS are baked into Rotur itself, such as origin's file system. Also because of this, originOS is usually seen as the example Rotur OS that people who make Rotur OSes take inspiration from.

The Rotur Team

Rotur has a team of 5 people (6 if you decide to count ThatBeaverDev).

Mist

Does most of the work, and is the only one with permissions to actually make changes to Rotur itself.

Flufi

The person who oversees Rotur's code quality, making sure it doesn't become too overloaded with AI-generated code. Flufi is also behind phosphorus - Rotur's version of the web, complete with its own 2 languages: RWL (HTML/CSS equivalent) and RTR (JS equivalent). As of the time this article is being written, phosphorus, RWL, and RTR are all still in development.

LayZ

Consultant. Not much is known about what he actually does for Rotur.

iris

Once re-did the main rotur.dev website to have better looks. Also helps out with other parts of Rotur every now and then.

MikeDEV

While Mike doesn't directly host Rotur, Rotur's websocket system, primarily used by services like Rmail / Omail, is based on the structure of CL4. At some point, Rotur used to run off of external CL4 servers, before Rotur moved to being fully self-hosted by Mistium.

ThatBeaverDev (Honorable Mention)

Came up with the name and idea for Rotur to begin with. He's basically the long lost grandfather of Rotur.

Rotur Services

See list of Rotur services

Rotur offers alternatives to several services that are offered by companies like Google or Microsoft, though because the creators of Rotur don't have nearly as many resources companies like Google or Microsoft do, storage for services like RoturPhotos is very limited unless you have a Rotur Subscription. As of now, most of the entire Rotur API is hosted off of one of the creators' spare MacBooks, which has ~1 TB of storage to work with.
