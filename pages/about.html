About

As you may have heard by now, Rotur Assistant is the ultimate, all-in-one utility tool for Rotur, which takes almost full advantage of what's available via the Rotur API. It even allows for some things you can't do through official managers, such as managing account keys outside of Discord/originChats, or managing gifts you created.

The idea

The idea for Rotur Assistant came from a few things:

  • Rotur Toolbox: A service that was similar to what Rotur Assistant is, that was planned by Milodev123. Unfortunately, it never got far in development.
  • roturBOT: Prior to Rotur Assistant, the only way to modify your account keys was through roturBOT, and if roturBOT goes down, then you were basically out of luck.
  • originOS: As good as originOS is, it is sometimes not the most reliable option for interacting with Rotur, since clicks have a chance of sometimes not registering. A workaround was discovered, where you tab out then tab back in to origin, but that would grant you like 3 free clicks before you had to repeat the process.

All in all, Rotur Assistant was made to allow users to have more control over their Rotur experience, free of any client-imposed bugs or limitations.

History

The idea for Rotur Assistant started a few months before development on Rotur Assistant officially started. I had the idea of making a Discord bot called "Rotur Assistant", which would've been basically an upgraded version of roturBOT, but thankfully I decided to settle on making it a browser extension, since you can do a lot more within a browser extension environment than a Discord bot (It would've been impossible to add the universal account switcher if I went down the Discord bot route). The name "Rotur Assistant" was inspired by how the popular Discord bot, Dank Memer, has a helper bot named Dank Assistant.

Development process

Development on Rotur Assistant started some time in late March. Since Rotur Assistant is my first full-fledged HTML/CSS/JS project, I learned some new things as I went along, such as new html tags like <details> and <section>. A good chunk of my HTML/CSS knowledge came from a college course I took about web design. I passed, though my general knowledge was still iffy. The web design course didn't cover JS at all, so I self-taught myself JS. I was already familiar with JS though, since I once made a Discord bot using node.js.

AI usage during development

I am proud to say, that most of the code for Rotur Assistant was hand-written. Claude AI was used to give the project a running start, and to teach me more about HTML/CSS/JS, but eventually, I learned enough to the point where I began working fully independent of AI. I still occasionally go back to it every now and then, especially when I need it to generate a calculation algorithm every once in a while. That or the occasional CSS error if I give up trying to debug it myself.
